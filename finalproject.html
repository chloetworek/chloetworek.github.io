CSC 106 Final Project

Welcome to Scavenge for Code! Written by me, Chloe, and Joshua Kinnes, this game was created for the final in URI's CSC 106 course. You can use it to work through questions about code and learn different terminology that can give you a jumpstart on your own creations. Show off with the knowledge you learn and continue to explore the world of code after you finish!

To start, go around the room and find a word with a corresponding image. Then, you can match that image to one of the ones in the program and type the word into the text box located right next to it (If you are not playing this when we present it in person, just use the list of word provided below). Now, after entering the word, you can click on the arrow button and proceed to one of the questions. Read the brief definition and then you should be ready to complete the practice question! If you get it right, you will be taken back to complete the next four. Once all are complete, congratulations! You have learned many basic coding terms and are now free to go off on your own, experimenting and learning more.
